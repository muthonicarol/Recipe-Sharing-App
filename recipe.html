Pilau

Pilau is a specialty for the Swahili people at the Kenyan coast. Pilau is now a common dish served during special occasions like weddings or other parties.

WHAT YOU NEED

1.One kilo of white rice - preferably 2.long-grained aromatic. 3.Half a kilo of goat meat - diced. Beef mutton or chicken will also do. 4.Freshly ground garlic. 5.Freshly ground ginger. 6.One large onion - sliced. 7.Two large tomatoes - sliced. (You could use tomato paste instead) 8.Cooking oil. 9.Pure ground pilau masala. 10.Half cup coconut milk - optional. 11.Whole potatoes - optional. 12.Dash of pepper and salt - optional. 13.Other spices - optional

HOW TO PREPARE PILAU

  1. Boil the meat until tender. Sprinkle some salt to taste. Once cooked, drain the remaining broth into a container and put aside. Add the fat and onions. Cook for a couple of minutes. Add freshly ground ginger and garlic then stir. Add the tomatoes or tomato paste. If you like, add the potatoes. Stir. Let it cook for five to 10 minutes. Add two level teaspoons of pure ground pilau masala and the dash of pepper. If you are using other spices, add them at this point. Stir. Let the spicy mixture simmer for about three minutes. Then add four to five cups of the meat broth. Top up with hot water or coconut milk if necessary. Wash the rice. After the water or broth has boiled, add the rice. Cook under low heat until all water has dried up or until ready.

Serving Tips

Pilau Must always be served dry not wet. Pilau goes well with Kachumbari, and a spice, thick soup on the side. Ripe Bananas are great for garnishing or as a dessert.

Chapati

Chapatis are undoubtedly the most loved dish in any Kenyan household. Children get excited when they learn that they are having chapattis for dinner. Special occasions are sometimes never special without chapatis on the menu.

WHAT YOU NEED

  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 3/4 cup vegetable oil,plus 1-3 tablespoons
  • 3/4 cup water (3/4 to 1)

HOW TO PREPARE CHAPATIS

  1. In a large bowl, combine salt and 2-1/2 cups flour. Add 1/4 cup oil and mix well. Add water little by little, stirring after each addition, until dough is soft. Knead dough in bowl for 5 to 10 minutes.
  2. Sprinkle about 1/4 cup flour on a flat surface. Take a 2-inch ball of dough and, with a floured rolling pin, roll out into a 1/8-inch-thick circle the size of a saucer. Repeat with remaining dough, sprinkling flat surface with flour if dough sticks.
  3. Heat 1 tablespoon oil over medium-high heat for 1 minute. Fry chapati 3 to 5 min- utes per side or until brown.
  4. Remove from pan and let drain on paper towels. Fry remaining chapatis, adding more oil if necessary.
  5. Serve immediately or place in a covered container until ready to serve.

SERVING TIPS

Chapati can be served with ndengu or beef stew

Matumbo

Matumbo in English is known as tripe, a name known to very few. It is the edible stomach lining/ intestines of farm animals especially cows

What You Need

  • One kilogram matumbo (tripe) which should include the blanket,the book, the honeycomb and pocket tripe.
  • Four large tomatoes, chopped
  • Two garlic cloves crushed
  • Two onions,chopped
  • One teaspoonful of curry powder
  • One cup coconut milk
  • One tablespoon tomato paste
  • One teaspoonful cooking oil
  • One bunch dhania(coriander)
  • Salt

HOW TO PREPARE MATUMBO

  1. Wash the matumbo thoroughly. For matumbo to be fit for human consumption it needs to be cleaned meticulously.
  2. Boil for one hour and drain the stock
  3. Add curry powder and stir briefly
  4. Add tomatoes and salt and cook till soft
  5. Add tomato paste and stir Add coconut milk and bring to boil
  6. Add the matumbo and stir. Cover and simmer for 15 minutes.
  7. Serve immediately while still hot

    8. Serving Tips

    Serve with Ugali, capati and Mukimo.

Madondo

What You Need

  • Quarter kilogramme beans
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • One chopped onion
  • One garlic clove chopped
  • One large tomato chopped
  • One tablespoon of curry powder
  • Salt to taste
  • A cup of fresh milk or coconut milk

    • HOW TO PREPARE MADONDO

    1. Select and wash the beans. Soaking the ndengu either overnight, or for a couple of hours can reduce cooking time.
    2. Boil the beans until its soft.
    3. Put aside and let it cool before mashing.
    4. In a separate pan, fry onions till tender.
    5. Add garlic and continue frying for two minutes.

Mukimo

Mukimo is a traditional Kikuyu dish. It is served at all major ceremonies especially weddings, funerals and fundraisings. A celebration is incomplete without this delicacy.

What You Need

  • Fresh green maize (shelled)
  • Kahurura(the tender young leaves of a pumpkin). Spinach is an alternative if pumpkin leaves are not available
  • Irish Potatoes
  • Butter or margarine

HOW TO PREPARE MUKIMO

  1. Boil green maize for at least two hours or until they are cooked.
  2. Add the peeled and diced potatoes but do not mix. Place the Kahurura on top of the potatoes after you have removed its stem and thoroughly washed the leaves.
  3. Sprinkle some salt
  4. Let the mixture boil for another 30 minutes to 45 minutes until the potatoes and the greens are well cooked. Remember, never stir this mixture otherwise you will make a mess out of your Mukimo.
  5. Once satisfied that the maize, potatoes and greens are well cooked, drain the remaining water and let the mixture simmer under low heat.
  6. Mix and mash the mixture. Add some margarine to give the Mukimo a delicate flavour. Ensure that the maize, potatoes and kahurura are evenly spread.
  7. The final product should have a very fine texture, is light green in colour and should neither be dry nor very moist. Some people like it when the maize remains intact(whole).

Serving Tips

Serve with Kachumbari,beef stew or some vegetable soup of your choice. Can also be served with tea in the morning.

Githeri

Apart from ugali, Githeri is also one of the main staple foods in Kenya. It is a mixture of maize and beans. There are variations of this mixture that may include adding peas, groundnuts, potatoes and vegetables.

What You Need

  • Five green maize removed from the cob
  • Half a kilogram of beans
  • Three peeled and sliced carrots
  • A quarter of small cabbage- washed and chopped
  • Three to four peeled and cubed potatoes
  • One and a half cups coconut milk
  • One big chopped onion
  • Two chopped tomatoes
  • Half a teaspoon of tumeric powder
  • One teaspoon of salt
  • One teaspoon of oil

HOW TO PREPARE Githeri

  1. Clean the beans and groundnuts then soak separately for 6 to 12 hours.
  2. Wash and boil the groundnuts for at least one hour.
  3. Wash the maize and beans and add into the pan with groundnuts. Cook for an hour.
  4. In another pan, fry onions till tender. Add tumeric powder and stir for two minutes.
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes and salt. Stir and cover with lid. Let it cook on low heat for three minutes.
  6. Add carrots and potatoes and stir.
  7. Add the coconut milk and let it cook on low heat for 15 minutes.

    8. Serving Tips

    The githeri stew can be served on its own or with beef stew and steamed cabbages.

Omena

Omena is Silver Cyprinid in English. they get their name from their silver color. It's a tiny freshwater fish found in lake Victoria. Omena is a favourite among the Luo community. If omena is not properly cleaned it acquires a bitter taste. Fishermen around lake Victoria dry the omena by the lakeside, before transporting it to the market. This makes the omena gather sand. You need to wash this sand off the omena. I will show you how to clean omena. If you follow this recipe, your omena will taste good.

What you need

  • Two cups of omena
  • Two large tomatoes
  • One large onion
  • Cooking oil
  • Salt
  • Water
  • Milk or coconut cream
  • Freshly ground garlic

How To Prepare Omena

  1. Boil water the add fish into it.
  2. Let the omena boil for 30 minutes to rid them of any dirt and scales they might have.
  3. Do not drain the water. Let the dirt settle at the bottom of the sufuria (sauce pan), then carefully scoop out the fish.
  4. Deep dry the fish for about two or three minutes or until they begin to brown.
  5. Put them in a bowl lined with paper towels to absorb the extra oil. This will also prevent breaking when they are being stewed.
  6. Heat oil in a pan and add the chopped onions
  7. Follow this with freshly grounf garlicand salt
  8. Cook for about three to five minutes in low heat while stirring
  9. Add chopped tomatoes and let them cook in the same low heat until ready.Stir to make thick paste.If tou are using any spices, add them at this point
  10. Add the deep fried omen to the mixture and stir gently
  11. Add either half a cup of hot water or milk or even coconut cream
  12. Let the simmer for about 10 minutes

Serving Tips

Good with Ugali add vegetables such as sukuma wiki, managu or kunde
